Synchronizing your main Outlook Calendar with Ibel is useful to keep track of your tasks and appointments, and across all related applications.

Register your application with the Microsoft identity platform

In order to be able to sync your Outlook Calendar with Ibelâ€™s Calendar, a Microsoft Azure account is needed. The creation of an account is free for users that have never tried or paid for Azure. For more information, click here.
Following, Set up an Azure AD Tenant, also called environment, which is a representation of an organization, to manage and register apps.
Ultimately, Register an Application, choosing the appropriate supported account type.

Note

When configuring your platform settings, choose Web.

For the Redirect URI restrictions, copy your Ibel database URI followed by /microsoft_account/authentication. Example:
For more information on the restrictions and limitations of URIs, check this page.

Note

Regarding credentials, you must Add a client secret, called Client Secret in Ibel, which allows Ibel to authenticate itself, requiring no interaction from your side. Certificates are optional.

Configuration in Ibel

Go to general Settings and activate the Outlook Calendar feature.

From your Azure portal, under Overview, copy your Application (client) ID, and paste it to Client ID in Ibel.

Going back to your Azure portal, now under Certificates & secrets, copy your Client Secret Value and paste it to Client Secret in Ibel.

Sync with Outlook

Important

Ibel Calendar sends a confirmation email at the creation of an event to its attendees. But, on the first sync, Outlook will also send confirmation emails of all scheduled events (including past ones).
To avoid this situation, we recommend archiving all past events, and removing yourself from future ones in Ibel Calendar before the first sync. This will prevent events from being created in Outlook, and therefore, no redundant emails will be sent during the first synchronization. After the sync, events will only send one confirmation email.

Go to the Calendar application and Sync with Outlook. You are asked to log in to your account, if you are not already, and grant the required permissions.

The synchronization is a two-way process, meaning that events are reconciled in both accounts (Outlook and Ibel).

Note

All users that want to use the synchronization simply need to sync their calendar with Outlook.
